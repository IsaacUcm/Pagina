Website Architecture

Content & structure blueprint

Site Navigation Structure

  • Home / Hero — clinic identity & brand promise
  • Our Team — anchor section for all profiles
  • Treatments A–Z — full indexed treatment list
  • Why Us — differentiators, philosophy & stats
  • Patient Resources — FAQs, aftercare guides
  • Book Appointment — persistent CTA in nav
  • Contact & Hours — map, directions, forms

Per-profile Content Points

  • Role badge + specialty descriptor
  • Anime avatar (consistent style, see guide)
  • Tabbed layout: About / Credentials / Treatments / Achievements
  • Bio in plain, empathetic language (2–3 paragraphs)
  • Academic chronology with institution + year
  • Certifications with issuing body
  • Indexed treatment tags (linkable)
  • Direct contact + booking CTA
  • Availability schedule

Trust-building Elements

  • Years-of-practice counter per doctor
  • Named professional awards & publications
  • Research contributions with DOI or citation
  • Clinic-level aggregate statistics strip
  • ITI / AACD / board body logos (with permission)
  • Department head leadership framing
  • Patient-oriented language throughout bios
  • Consistent avatar style signals team cohesion

Anime Avatar Design Guidelines

Clean-line anime style (not chibi, not hyper-realistic). Vector SVG, solid fills only. Think Spy × Family-level professionalism — expressiveness without caricature.

All avatars use the same canvas (140×140px), body position (three-quarter frontal), lab coat or scrubs base, and stethoscope prop. Only face, hair, and sidebar colour change per doctor.

Warm skin tones (not race-coded). Hair colours stay within natural dark ranges. No culturally-specific accessories. Eyes should be warm and approachable — avoid very pale eyes that can read as cold.

Calm-but-warm default expression with a slight smile. Feature geometry (eye size, jaw width) varies subtly between avatars to differentiate — avoid making gender the primary differentiator; rely on hair and style instead.
